Nintendo has made many consoles throughout the years. I will be talking about five of the consoles Nintendo has made and their milestones they have achieved with it.

Nintendo Entertainment System

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_video_game_consoles

The Nintendo Entertainment System, or the NES, was released in July 15, 1983, and was one of Nintendo's first home video game to be released outside of Japan. This was the second generation console that Nintendo has ever made. The NES was one of the bestselling consoles at that time, selling 61.91 million units worldwide. It also helped revitalize the video game industry due to the incident of the video game crash of 1983 and set standards for consoles from game design to business practices. It also introduced to some big game franchises like Super Mario Brothers, The Legend of Zelda, and Metroid. It was progressively released for many years with different bundles such as the Deluxe set, the Basic Set, The Action Set, and the Power Set. The controllers for the NES were hard-wired to the console and had no connectors for cost reasons. Its designs were reused from the Game & Watch machines, although they originally wanted to use arcade-style joysticks. There were some concerns of the joystick's durability design and that children might step on the controller. They then attached a Game & Watch D-pad for the prototype and found it was easy to use and caused no discomfort. In August 14, 1995, Nintendo discontinued the NES in both the North America and Europe. Replacements for the original NES were available for $25 in exchange for a broken system until around December 1996, under Nintendo's Power Swap program. The Famicom was discontinued in September 2003 and Nintendo offered repair service to it until 2007 when it was discontinued due to the shortage of available parts.

Super Nintendo Entertainment System

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_video_game_consoles

The Super Nintendo Entertainment System, also known as the Super NES or SNES was a 16-bit video game console that was released in November 21,1990. It was the Nintendo's third home console and second console to release outside of Japan. It was a global success that outmatched its predecessors popularity in Northeast Asia and North America. The SNES gained positive reviews and became a global success, selling 49.1 million units and becoming the best-selling consoles of the 16-bit era. It also remained pretty popular during the 32-bit era as well. It also started some popular franchises like Mega Man X and Star Fox. In Japan, the SNES was actually called the Super Famicom, and in South Korea, it was called the Super Comboy and was distributed by the Hyundai Electronics. The SNES controller added a X and Y button to their design with their original A and B buttons, arranging all four buttons into a diamond shape. They had also added shoulder buttons to their controller. All of the models of the SNES were were predominantly grey and had a boxy design with purple sliding switches and dark grey eject lever. It was able to produce 1,757 games globally and most of them were able to be ported into the Game Boy Advance, which had similar video capabilities. Today most of the SNES games can be emulated. Although it could not repeat on to the success as the NES was, the Super NES was one of the best selling consoles in its era.

Nintendo 64

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_video_game_consoles

The Nintendo 64, also known was the N6 and codenamed Ultra 64, was Nintendo's fourth home console they ever made and was their third console to be released outside of Japan. It was also the last major home console to use cartridges as its primary storage format until the Nintendo switch in 2017. It competed with Playstation and the Sega Saturn as it was the fifth-generation console. It was able to sell 32.3 million units globally. It's development began in 1993 with partnering with Silicon Graphics, using the codename Project Reality. Their final design was named after its 64 CPU that helped to to the console's 3D capabilities. Their launch was delayed due to the development of the launch games Super Mario 64, Pilotwings 64, and Saikyo Habu Shogi. The console was critically acclaimed and is still one of the most recognized video game consoles. Their controller of the N64 was shaped as an "M" and features 10 buttons, 1 analog control stick, and a directional pad. The Nintendo 64 was one of the first gaming consoles to have four controller ports because it was the first console that could handle a four player split screen without significant slowdown. At its first release, it had heavy demand, selling 350,000 to 500,000 consoles in just three days. The console was able to produce 393 games prior to the release of the console. Some of their games like Super Mario 64, Super Smash bros, and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time are widely considered by critics "the most greatest and influential games of all time"

Nintendo Gamecube

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_video_game_consoles

The Nintendo Gamecube, shortened as Gamecube, NGC, or the GCN was released in November 5, 2001. It was Nintendo's sixth generation console, which was in the same generation as Sega's Dreamcast, Sony's Playstation 2, and Microsoft's Xbox. Their design project was known as the Dolphin. The Gamecube was the most compact sixth generation console, being Nintendo's first game console to use optical discs than game cartridges. The Gamecube was able to sell 21.74 million units globally. They also made hit franchises like Super Mario Sunshine, Metroid Prime, and Pokemon Colosseum. They introduced to some new franchises like Animal Crossing and Pikmin. After the N64's three handed controller, they learned the positives and negatives and made the controller of the Gamecube to two-handed. The shape of their controller was popular by Sony's Playstation controller released in 1994. Nintendo made similar features of their controller from the sixth generation consoles, but changed how the analog sticks and d-pad were positioned. The controller featured eight buttons, two analog sticks, a d-pad, and an internal rumble motor. Their console was shaped like a Cube with a handle on it. They had eight games that were supported by network connectivity and three with local area network. Its best selling game was Super smash bros melee, which sold 7.09 copies as of March 10, 2008. It sold 280,000 to 300,000 in the first three days in Japan. In February 2007, Nintendo announced their discontinuation with the Gamecube, as they were trying to shift to develop and manufacture their efforts towards the Wii and Nintendo DS.

Wii

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_video_game_consoles

The Wii was released in November 19, 2006 as Nintendo's sixth home console they ever made. It was also apart of the seventh generation of consoles. They designed the console to appeal wider audiences than their main competitors which were the Playstation 3 and Xbox 360, including casual players that were new to video games. This was emphasized by its distinguishing feature, which was the Wii Remote. It was a handheld motion controller that can detect motion and rotation in three dimensions. Within a year of the launch of the Wii, it became the sales leader against other seventh generation consoles and surpassed over 100 millions units by 2013. Their total lifetime sales for the Wii had reached over 103.1 million, making it the their best selling home console and as of 2021, it is the fourth best selling home console to date. It also featured internet enabled features, which allowed supported games to be able to offer online Multiplayer and other features. The wii was also able to feature backward compatibility with Gamecube games, storage, and controllers. Copies of wii games were shaped like a DVD and were packaged in keep cases with instructions. The wii was also introduced to the use of Miis, which were player customized avatars.1,595 games were produced for the wii during its time. The Wii helped Nintendo get repositioned as a key player in the video game console marketplace. Their introduction of motion-controlled games with the Wii remote led both Microsoft and Sony to develop their own competing products like the Kinect and Playstation Move.
